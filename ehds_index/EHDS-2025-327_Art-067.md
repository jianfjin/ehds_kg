# Art. 67 — Anonymisation of electronic health data

## Para 1
Where electronic health data are made available for secondary use in an anonymous format, the requirements laid down in Articles 54(2) and 59(1)(b) shall not apply.

## Para 2
Anonymisation shall be carried out in accordance with the implementing acts adopted pursuant to Article 68, ensuring that the data are rendered anonymous in such a manner that the data subject is no longer identifiable.

## Para 3
The health data access body shall verify that the anonymisation techniques applied meet the standards referred to in paragraph 2 before making the data available.

## TEHDAS2 Implementation Guidance (M7.2)

Anonymisation under Art. 67 is not a binary or permanent status. Previously anonymised data may, in the future, cease to meet the conditions for anonymity due to technological advances or the ability to combine multiple datasets (GDPR Recital 26 — "means reasonably likely to be used").

Key TEHDAS2 recommendations:
- HDABs must apply tested state-of-the-art techniques: generalisation, suppression, randomisation (Recital 65 EHDS).
- Privacy risk assessments must cover: re-identification risk, attribute inference, membership inference, and linkage attacks.
- All anonymisation activities must be thoroughly documented for transparency and accountability.
- When data are made available in anonymous format, Arts. 54(2) and 59(1)(b) do not apply — simplified access pathway.
- Synthetic data generation is a related but distinct concept (not mandated by EHDS; HDABs may support via evaluation frameworks).
- Risk mitigation techniques for quasi-identifiers: generalisation (e.g., age brackets), suppression (e.g., small cell counts), randomisation (noise injection — use carefully as it reduces data utility).
