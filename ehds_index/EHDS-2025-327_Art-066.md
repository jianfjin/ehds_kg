# Art. 66 — Penalties

## Para 1
Member States shall lay down the rules on penalties applicable to infringements of this Regulation and shall take all measures necessary to ensure that they are implemented.

## Para 2
The penalties provided for shall be effective, proportionate and dissuasive.

## Para 3
Member States shall, without delay, notify the Commission of those rules and of any subsequent amendment affecting them.

## Para 4
Penalties may include:
  (a) administrative fines;
  (b) temporary or permanent bans on processing;
  (c) orders to cease and desist;
  (d) corrective measures to ensure compliance.

## TEHDAS2 Guidance (M4.1.2 Penalties Guideline)

The TEHDAS2 M4.1.2 guideline provides practical operationalisation of Article 66 alongside Articles 63 and 64:

### Enforcement Measures Available (Article 63)
- **Periodic penalty payments** for data holders who obstruct access or miss deadlines (Article 60(2))
- **Revocation of data permits** with immediate cessation of processing + SPE access credential disabling
- **Exclusion from future access** for up to 5 years for serious/repeated/systemic non-compliance
- **Remedial measures**: technical/organisational safeguards, enhanced oversight, formal undertakings

### Administrative Fine Tiers (Article 64)
- **Less serious** (procedural breaches under Articles 60/61): up to €10M or 2% worldwide turnover
- **More serious** (unauthorised processing, re-identification attempts, obstruction of HDAB): up to €20M or 4% worldwide turnover
- **Multiple infringements**: total fine capped at the most serious single infringement maximum
- **Repeat/violation after warning**: aggravating factor for stricter sanctions

### Procedural Safeguards
- Notification with evidence to non-compliant party (max 4 weeks to respond)
- Right to be heard before enforcement decision finalised
- Access to effective judicial remedies under national law
- Coordination with GDPR supervisory authorities where personal data breaches also involved
- Enforcement measures must be published on HDAB websites (Art. 57(1)(j)(iv)) and notified through HealthData@EU IT tool

### Cross-Border Enforcement and Forum Shopping Prevention
The TEHDAS2 M4.1.2 guideline emphasizes that cross-border enforcement scenarios require particular attention:
- If a data user from Member State A commits a serious breach inside an SPE in Member State B, questions arise on which HDAB holds enforcement competence — both may need to act in coordination
- Exclusion decisions (temporary bans up to 5 years) taken by one HDAB must be systematically notified to all other HDABs via the HealthData@EU IT tool to prevent **forum shopping** — non-compliant users seeking access in other Member States
- Until mutual recognition mechanisms or interoperability rules are established, the risk of regulatory fragmentation remains

### HealthData@EU IT Tool Publication Requirements
- Enforcement decisions must be entered into the shared IT tool **within 10 working days** of adoption
- Subsequent updates should occur on a **monthly** schedule to ensure accuracy
- If a decision is overturned on appeal or by judicial review, the HDAB must **promptly mark it as inactive or withdraw** it from the IT tool
- HDABs must publish summaries of enforcement measures on their websites (Art. 57(1)(j)(iv))

### Commission Guidelines Timeline
- The Commission will issue detailed guidelines on enforcement measures by **26 March 2032** (Art. 63(8)), in cooperation with the EHDS Board
- These will cover: periodic penalty payments, revocation/exclusion criteria, and enforcement methodologies
- Until then, HDABs should align with national administrative law frameworks
