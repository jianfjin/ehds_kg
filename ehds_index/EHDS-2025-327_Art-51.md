# Article 51 — Administrative fines for infringements of specific provisions

## Index Summary
> **Stable ID:** `EHDS-2025-327-A51`  
> **Regulation:** (EU) 2025/327  
> **Article:** 51  
> **Category:** Chapter V — Secondary Use

## Para 1
[[A51-P1]]

1. Infringements of the provisions concerning the general principles for processing, the conditions for consent, and the rights of data subjects shall be subject to administrative fines up to EUR 10 000 000 or 2 % of the total worldwide annual turnover.
2. Infringements of the provisions concerning the obligations of data controllers and processors shall be subject to administrative fines up to EUR 20 000 000 or 4 % of the total worldwide annual turnover.

## TEHDAS2 Guidance (M4.1.2 Penalties Guideline)

Article 51 establishes the two-tier fine structure for specific EHDS infringements, which the TEHDAS2 M4.1.2 guideline operationalises through Articles 63 and 64:

### Fine Tiers Under EHDS
- **Tier 1 (Lower):** Infringements of provisions on general processing principles, consent conditions, and data subject rights → up to **€10,000,000 or 2%** of total worldwide annual turnover
- **Tier 2 (Higher):** Infringements of provisions on obligations of data controllers and processors → up to **€20,000,000 or 4%** of total worldwide annual turnover

### Operationalisation via Articles 63-64
The M4.1.2 guideline details how HDABs implement these fine tiers in practice:
- **Less serious infringements** (procedural/cooperation failures under Art. 60-61): align with Tier 1 ceilings — e.g. failure to fulfil procedural obligations by data holders or data users
- **More serious infringements** (unauthorised processing, re-identification attempts, obstruction): align with Tier 2 ceilings — violations that directly undermine core EHDS principles
- HDABs must assess on a case-by-case basis using criteria: nature/gravity/duration, intentionality/negligence, mitigation efforts, cooperation level, and financial benefit obtained

### Key Principles
- Fines must be **effective, proportionate, and dissuasive** (mirroring Article 66(2))
- Multiple infringements: total fine capped at the maximum for the most serious single infringement (Art. 64(5))
- Procedural safeguards: right to be heard (max 4 weeks per Art. 63(2)), access to judicial remedies, coordination with DPAs

### Cross-Border and Forum Shopping Considerations
- If a data user commits a breach in one Member State's SPE, enforcement competence questions arise — both the host and home HDABs may need to coordinate
- Exclusion decisions (up to 5-year bans) must be notified to all HDABs via HealthData@EU IT tool to prevent **forum shopping** — non-compliant users seeking data access in other Member States
- Until mutual recognition mechanisms are established, cross-border enforcement gaps remain a risk

### Coordination with GDPR Framework
- Enforcement powers under EHDS complement but do NOT replace GDPR supervisory authorities
- Where non-compliance involves potential personal data breaches, HDABs must inform and coordinate with the competent DPA without delay
- All relevant documentation — findings, supporting evidence — must be forwarded to the DPA

### Timeline and Future Developments
- The Commission will issue detailed guidelines on enforcement measures by **26 March 2032** (Art. 63(8))
- Enforcement decisions should be entered into the HealthData@EU IT tool within **10 working days** and updated **monthly**
- Decisions overturned on appeal must be promptly marked as inactive or withdrawn
